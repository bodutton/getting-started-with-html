HTML is written in sentences or <em>elements</em> composed of <em>tags </em>and text. <b>Tags</b> are the markers that tell the text or source how to act or look. There's a tag for anything you want to accomplish just a quick google search away. All tags fall into one of two categories, inline or block. These categorizations describe how the tag relates to the text and how it's laid out on the page. Most tags need an opening tag and a closing tag.</p><br> <br> Beginners Tags:<br> <br> <b>Inline</b><br> v b v = Bold<br> v em v = Italics<br> v a v = Anchor (add hyperlinks, needs attribute like href)<br> v img v = Image (no closing tag, needs source and alt text)<br> v br v = Line Break (no closing tag)<br> v q v = Short Quotation<br> v strong v = Defines Important Text<br> <br> <b>Block</b><br> v p v = Paragraph (can give heigth and width)<br> v body v = Body<br> v form v = User Input Field<br> <br> <br> <h4>HTML Document Structure</h4> - Doctype Tag - v !doctype html v<br> - HTML opening tag - v html v <br> - head opening tag - v head v<br> - title - v title v TITLE! v /title v<br> - /head closing tag - v /head v<br> - body opening tag - v body v<br> - majority of content - v b v content v /b v<br> - body closing tag - v /body v<br> - /HTML closing tag - v /html v<br>
